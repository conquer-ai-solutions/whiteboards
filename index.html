01
Context
Create space. Let them talk. Don't fill the silence.
This beat takes 15–40 minutes in every session that works. The client should be speaking 80% of the time. Your job is to track what's emerging, not to respond to it yet.
15–40 min · Client talks · You listen & tag
Signal tracker — tag what you're hearing
Click to mark. These surface your Beat 3 frame.
Process signals
Manual, repetitive work being done at volume
Process that depends on one person's knowledge
Handoffs where things get lost or delayed
Same transformation happening the same way every time
Workaround that has become the official process
Commercial signals
Revenue or pipeline moving slower than it should
High-performers doing work below their level
Decisions being made without good enough data
Compliance or risk exposure that's managed manually
Live notes — their words, not yours
The one thing this beat does
It gives you the raw material for Beat 3. Every concept you deploy in Synthesis has to be earned by something the client just said. If they haven't said it yet, you can't use it yet.

02
Probe
Surgical questions. Not a grid. Follow what they said.
These aren't asked in sequence — they're deployed responsively, when the context earns them. Each one is designed to unlock the thing the previous answer didn't surface.
10–20 min · Responsive, not sequential · Follow the thread
Q1
If your highest performer left tomorrow — what would fall apart first?
Surfaces institutional knowledge risk and undocumented process. The answer tells you exactly where the AI opportunity is hiding.
Listen for: a specific name, a specific task, "only they know how to..." — that's your target.
Q2
What was the last thing your team was doing at 6pm on a Friday that they shouldn't have been?
Cuts through the idealised version of process. Gets to the real high-frequency, high-friction work hiding in plain sight.
Listen for: the exact task, the exact day/time, the feeling of resignation in the answer — that's where effort is leaking.
Q3
Are these transformations procedural — do they happen the same way every time?
The most important technical qualifier. If yes, it's automatable. Appeared almost word-for-word in both Lingotto and Rumo sessions.
Listen for: "mostly" or "it depends" — those caveats define the actual scope boundary.
Q4
Where do things fall through the cracks — and who catches them?
Reveals handoff failures and the informal QA layer. The person who "catches them" is often doing a second full job that nobody's counting.
Listen for: a single name mentioned twice — that's your hidden dependency and your strongest use case.
Q5
What decision do you make every week that you don't have good enough data for?
Opens the data quality and decision-support conversation without sounding like a BI pitch. Gets to the real information gap and its downstream cost.
Listen for: hesitation before the answer — what they pause on is what they care about most.
Q6 — Close beat
What would have to be true for you to say, six months from now, that this was the best decision you made this year?
Sets success criteria in their words. Creates shared accountability. Used to anchor Beat 5 — this answer is what you validate against before you close.
Listen for: the emotional language, not just the metrics. "My team stops dreading Monday" is more useful than "20% efficiency gain."
⚡ Instinct probes — from the transcripts, used in multiple sessions
"Who actually touches this, and how many times?" — used at Goodman, Carter's, Rumo. Always surfaces scope.
"What's the consequence when it goes wrong?" — the answer frames the commercial case without you having to make it.
"Is this a people problem, a process problem, or a data problem?" — used at News UK. Lets them diagnose before you do.
"If you had this working perfectly, what would you do with the time back?" — reveals strategic ambition above the use case.
When to open this: They've asked "what are you seeing in the landscape?" or the session has flipped and they want your view, not yours of theirs. Lead with conviction. Name patterns from real engagements. Don't pitch — think out loud together.
🎯
What's actually working right now
The highest-value implementations are extraction-first — unstructured data (documents, emails, calls) being turned into structured, queryable, actionable data. Not chatbots. Not co-pilots. Data pipelines that replace hours of manual work with seconds of automated processing.
⚠️
What's overhyped right now
Autonomous agents that operate without human oversight in production. The models aren't reliable enough for fully unsupervised decisions in regulated or high-stakes environments yet. The value is in human-in-the-loop automation — AI drafts, routes, flags; human decides on exceptions.
📈
Where the landscape is moving fast
Model costs dropping 10x every 12–18 months. What's expensive to run today will be near-free in 2 years. The companies building the capability now will have a two-year head start on deploying it cheaply at scale. The architecture decisions made today determine whether you can take advantage of that.
🔑
The pattern we see across every successful engagement
The organisations winning aren't the ones with the biggest AI budgets — they're the ones who started with a specific, bounded, high-frequency problem and solved it completely before expanding. Every failed implementation we've seen tried to boil the ocean first.
When to open this: The opening probes have surfaced a specific process worth going deep on — "vendor chasing", "the weekly Excel", "the compliance sign-off." Now map it systematically. Go dimension by dimension. Each answer directly informs whether and how Conquer can solve it.

03
Synthesis
Name what they just described — in Conquer's language.
This is the highest-value moment in every session. The concepts below are mirrors, not openers. Deploy each one only when the client has already described their own version of it. The words "what you're describing is exactly..." are your bridge.
10–15 min · Responsive framing · Only deploy what's been earned
🪞
The bridge phrase
"What you're describing is exactly what [concept] looks like in practice." — When you can say this genuinely, the concept lands. When you have to force it, the client feels it. Only use these cards when the client has given you something to reflect back.
Deploy when earned
The Agentic Shift
AI as a tool you use vs. AI as a system that works. Agents that monitor, decide, and act without being asked. Connected across your systems. Proactive by design.
Trigger condition
"What you're describing — where [their process] requires someone to manually trigger it every time — is exactly what the Agentic Shift looks like in practice."
Deploy when earned
Process before automation
We design the right process first, then automate it. Most AI projects fail because they automate broken processes. The workaround they just described is exactly that broken process.
Trigger condition
"What you're describing — [their workaround] becoming the standard approach — is exactly why we spend time on process design before we write any code."
Deploy when earned
The institutional knowledge risk
What lives in people's heads, not in systems. When that person leaves — and they will — the knowledge leaves with them. Capture it as structured, queryable, automatable process.
Trigger condition
"When you described [person] being the only one who knows how to do [task] — that's the exact risk we're designed to eliminate."
Deploy when earned
The compounding cost
The cost isn't just the task. It's the opportunity cost of the person doing it. High performers doing automatable work aren't just costing time — they're costing the work they should be doing instead.
Trigger condition
"[Name] spending [X hours] on [task] — that's not just the hours. It's whatever [Name] would have done with those hours instead."
Deploy when earned
The differentiation window
What Conquer builds for you can't be bought off a shelf. In 18 months, your specific implementation will be a competitive advantage your competitors are trying to catch up to.
Trigger condition
When they mention a competitor or industry pressure: "The firms building this now are the ones setting the standard. That window is real."
Deploy when earned
The at-risk model
Discovery at our risk. Pilot at our risk. You pay only when working software meets agreed criteria. Not a tactic — a statement of confidence. First engagement, no upfront cost.
Trigger condition
When risk or budget comes up: "We remove the financial risk from your side entirely. The only thing you're risking is the time in the room today."
